# FlightSchedule — Design Context

This file is the source of truth for how FlightSchedule should look and feel. It is loaded by design-focused skills (`/normalize`, `/polish`, `/critique`, `/frontend-design`, etc.) and should be respected by every visual change.

## Design Context

### Users

**Who:** ~5–12 private pilots and 1–2 admins of a French aeroclub that shares a single Cessna 182 (F-GBQA). Closed user group, no public sign-up. Pilots skew older (40s–70s typical for private GA in France) and trained but not necessarily tech-savvy.

**Context of use:** Almost always on a phone, often outdoors at the airfield in bright sunlight, sometimes with one hand or a clipboard in the other. Occasionally on desktop at home for purchases or admin tasks. Network can be patchy at the field.

**The job to be done:** Replace a fragmented Sheet+WhatsApp+paper+Excel workflow with a calm, single source of truth. Pilots need to: see their HDV (flight-hour) balance at a glance, book the aircraft without conflicts, log a flight in under a minute, and trust the numbers. Admins need to: validate flights, manage pilots, and configure availability without ceremony.

**Emotional goal:** Confidence and ease. The app should feel like a well-kept logbook in your pocket — precise where it matters (numbers, status, time), warm where it can be (greetings, empty states, the moments around the aircraft itself).

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Warm. Clear. Welcoming.

**Voice:** French only, V1. Speak like a fellow pilot, not a SaaS dashboard — never marketing-speak, never patronizing, never robotic. Short sentences. Tu/vous: keep the existing French copy in `src/lib/copy.ts` consistent (do not invent your own register without checking). When uncertain, prefer the warmer-but-still-precise phrasing.

**Tone of numbers:** HDV is sacred. Always `HH:MM`, never decimals. Numbers should feel as legible as a panel gauge — big, monospaced where it matters, never ambiguous.

**Tone of status:** Honest and immediate. Pending vs validated, available vs unavailable, confirmed vs cancelled — these states should be unmistakable at a glance, with both color *and* an icon or label (never color alone — see Design Principles).

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual identity:** *Friendly aeroclub* — light-first, sky-blue accented, calm and welcoming with the precision of real aviation tooling underneath. Imagine the warmth of an Apple Wallet card married to the disciplined semantic color of a Garmin G1000 panel. Light mode is the canonical experience; dark mode is a polite alternative that should not regress in polish but is the secondary surface.

**Reference touchstones:**
- **Garmin G1000 / ForeFlight** — for *color discipline* and *glanceable status*. Semantic colors (green/amber/red) carry meaning, never decoration. Large numerics where they matter. Information density without clutter.
- **Apple system apps (Wallet, Health, Fitness)** — for *softness and rhythm*. Generous whitespace, soft rounded cards, friendly typography hierarchy, large hero numbers, mobile-first compositions, clear empty states. Warm but never toy-like.

**Color direction:** Sky / aviation blues as the primary brand thread. Pull a confident, daylight-clear blue (think the open-sky posture of CAVOK weather — ceiling and visibility OK) as the primary accent. Neutrals stay warm — prefer warm-grays/stone over cool zinc. Reserve the existing semantic palette (emerald / amber / red) for HDV balance tiers and status states **only**, never as decoration.

**Anti-references — what FlightSchedule must NOT look like:**
- **Generic SaaS / AI startup** — no purple gradients, no glassmorphism, no abstract hero shapes, no "powered by AI" energy.
- **Enterprise / legacy admin** — no Bootstrap-era tables, no beige forms, no dense gray ERP look, no '90s admin panel chrome.
- **Toy / cartoonish** — no mascots, no oversaturated brights, no gamification badges. This is a real aircraft and a real ledger of money.
- **Cold / corporate** — no sterile Office-blue, no faceless fleet-operator vibes. This is a community of friends who own a plane together, not a procurement portal.

### Design Principles

These five principles override convenience. When a design decision is unclear, walk through them in order.

1. **Numbers are the hero.** HDV balances, durations, dates, and statuses are why pilots open the app. They get the largest type, the highest contrast, the most generous spacing, and tabular/monospaced numerics where alignment matters. Decoration never competes with data.

2. **Glanceable, then explorable.** A pilot pulling up the dashboard at the airfield in sunlight should know their balance, their next reservation, and the state of their last flight in under two seconds — without scrolling, without parsing. Detail lives one tap deeper.

3. **Color carries meaning, never mood.** Green / amber / red are reserved for HDV tiers and status states. Sky blue is reserved for the brand and primary actions. Never color a button "for variety." Never rely on color alone — every semantic color is paired with an icon or text label so that color blindness, sunlight glare, and grayscale screens never strand a user.

4. **Mobile-first is literal, not a buzzword.** Every screen is designed for a phone held one-handed at the airfield first, then enhanced upward at `md:`/`lg:`. Touch targets ≥ 44px. Numeric inputs surface the numeric keyboard. Forms validate server-side. Daylight contrast is non-negotiable — no pale-on-pale gray combinations.

5. **Warm where you can, precise where you must.** Greetings, empty states, micro-copy, and the spaces around the aircraft (the "we" of the aeroclub) can breathe and smile. Money, hours, validations, and bookings stay precise, honest, and unembellished. The two registers coexist; never let warmth blur a number, never let precision chill a welcome.

## Operational Notes for AI Tools

- **French only.** All user-facing copy must be in French. Do not introduce English strings. Reuse `src/lib/copy.ts` rather than inlining new copy.
- **Tailwind 4 CSS-first.** There is no `tailwind.config.js`. Theme tokens live in `@theme inline { ... }` inside `src/app/globals.css`. Add new design tokens there, not in JS.
- **Existing primitives live in `src/components/ui/`** (`Button`, `Card`, `Input`, `Label`, `Badge`, `Dialog`). Extend or refine these rather than introducing parallel components.
- **Semantic balance tiers** are defined once in `src/lib/duration.ts` (`balanceTier`, `BALANCE_TIER_CLASSES`). Reuse them; never re-derive.
- **Icons:** `lucide-react` is the only icon set. Stay consistent with its line weight.
- **Font:** Inter via `next/font/google`, exposed as `--font-sans`. If a tabular numeric variant is wanted for HDV displays, enable `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums` rather than swapping fonts.
- **PWA assets** (`/public/icon-192.png`, `/icon-512.png`, `/apple-icon.png`, `favicon.ico`, `logo.png`) are already in place — match the brand color you derive from `logo.png`.
